Data Science & Business Analytics Trends in 2024

Introduction

The demand for data science, business analytics, and machine learning professionals has continued to grow as organizations increasingly rely on data-driven decision making. At the same time, rapid advances in artificial intelligence have transformed skill requirements and created new opportunities across industries.

Using the Lightcast job postings dataset, this project investigates labor market trends from 2024. Specifically, we examine the most in-demand skills, the growing importance of AI and machine learning expertise, the industries hiring the largest number of data professionals, and the overall career outlook for business analytics practitioners.

Research Rationale

Understanding labor market trends is important for students and professionals preparing for careers in data science and business analytics. Analyzing job postings provides valuable insights into employer expectations, skill requirements, salary patterns, and geographic demand.

In addition to exploratory data analysis, this project incorporates findings from external sources such as OECD, Stanford HAI, Lightcast reports, and academic literature. By combining labor market data with existing research, the project provides a broader understanding of how artificial intelligence is reshaping workforce demand and influencing the future of analytics-related careers.

Data Analysis

To begin data exploration and analysis, the dataset was put through comprehensive data cleaning. The second research question could then be properly examined. By finding the most frequently observed AI-related terms in the dataset, the data revealed the increase in AI-related language. The current job market demand is seen to have a combination of traditional AI/ML concepts alongside newer generative AI terms.

EDA Analysis

Chart 1: Job Postings by Industry

Why: Identifies where hiring demand is concentrated, helping job seekers know which industries to target.

Insights: Unclassified Industry dominates, followed by Custom Computer Programming Services and Administrative Management. This suggests strong demand in tech and business services sectors.

Chart 2: Salary Distribution by Industry

Why: Reveals not just average pay but salary spread and outliers within each industry.

Insights: Salaries across most industries cluster between 100𝑘−200k. Offices of Certified Public Accountants shows the tightest range, while Employment Placement Agencies and Temporary Help Services have extreme outliers pushing past 400𝑘−500k, likely reflecting contract or senior placements. Computer Systems Design Services has the most data points concentrated at the lower end, suggesting a high volume of entry to mid-level roles.

Chart 3: Remote vs. On-site

Why: Remote work flexibility is a key factor for job seekers evaluating opportunities.

Insights: 78% of postings have no remote type specified ([NONE]), which likely means on-site or unspecified. Only 17.2% are fully remote, 3.12% hybrid, and 1.56% explicitly not remote. This suggests that true remote flexibility remains limited, and most employers either require in-person work or do not advertise remote options at all.

Chart 4: Top In-Demand Skills

Why: Directly answers our research question on what skills DS/BA/ML roles require most.

Insights: Communication and Data Analysis are the top two skills by a significant margin, followed by Management and SQL Python appears further down the list, alongside Tableau and Dashboard skills. This suggests employers value a blend of soft skills and technical tools, not just coding ability.

Chart 5: Top Job Titles

Why: Shows which roles are actually being hired for, giving a realistic picture of the job market.

Insights: Data Analysts dominate postings by a wide margin with over 8,000 listings, nearly triple the next category. Business Intelligence Analysts and Enterprise Architects follow, with many specialized analyst roles (Data Governance, Data Quality, Data Modelers) also appearing. This indicates strong and diverse demand across the data field.

Chart 6: Salary By Job Title (Top 10 Titles)

Why: Shows earning potential across the most common roles to help with career planning.

Insights: Data Analytics Engineers command the highest salaries with a median around $200k and a wide range, while Data Analysts sit at the lower end around $100k-$150k despite being the most posted role. Enterprise Architects and Oracle Cloud HCM Consultants also show strong earning potential with notable outliers. This suggests that specialization is rewarded significantly over generalist analyst roles.

Chart 7: Job Postings By State

Why: Geographic variation in job postings helps job seekers understand where to target their search and whether relocation could significantly expand opportunities.

Insights: Texas and California dominate by a wide margin, together accounting for a large share of all postings. Florida, Virgina, Illinois, and New York follow, suggesting that major tech and business hubs remain the primary hiring centers.

Skill Gap Analysis

The team compiled a list of technical skills that they believed would be in high demand prior to conducting exploratory analysis. Upon reviewing the EDA results, the list was updated to include the top 10 in-demand skills. The team used their academic and professional experience to assign each skill with a proficiency level. The comparison of individual proficiencies and job market demand revealed possible areas of improvement.

Advanced Visualizations

Top 10 Skills Ranked by Month (2024) Filter by Job Title - Bump Chart

Why This Chart?

A bump chart shows how skill rankings shift over time across five months (May–September 2024). Filtering with the dropdown shows which skills are consistently prioritized vs. situationally demanded for each job title. This is something a static bar chart cannot reveal.

Key Insights

  • Most job titles show significant rank volatility across the five months, suggesting that employer skill priorities shift frequently within a hiring cycle rather than remaining fixed.
  • The same skill can rank #1 in one month and drop to #5 or lower the next, indicating that no single skill dominates consistently for most roles.
  • Technical titles like Data Analysts tend to cluster around data-related skills (SQL, Data Analysis, Power BI) in the top ranks, while management-adjacent titles show more varied and unstable rankings.
  • The May–July period tends to show the most movement, while August–September rankings appear slightly more stable, possibly reflecting end-of-year hiring consolidation.

Implications for Job Seekers

Use the dropdown to find your target job title and identify which skills hold the top 3 ranks consistently, those are your must-haves. Skills that fluctuate between ranks 4–10 are strong differentiators but not dealbreakers. Prioritize stable top-ranked skills in your resume before adding situational ones.

Skills Landscape by Industry (Interactive) - Treemap

Why This Chart?

This interactive version allows job seekers to focus on a single industry at a time, reducing visual noise and enabling direct comparison of role and skill demand within a specific sector.

Key Insights

  • Management Consulting shows the broadest skill variety, with Databricks, ServiceNow, and Tealium appearing alongside standard tools. This reflects hybrid technical-business roles.
  • Computer Systems Design heavily favors Microsoft Access and Progress Reporting for Data Analysts, suggesting a more traditional enterprise analytics stack.
  • Public Accountants is the smallest sector but shows BigQuery and Capital Markets skills, indicating emerging data modernization in accounting firms.

Industry → Job Title → Skills Flow - Sankey

Why This Chart?

A Sankey diagram visualizes flow and volume simultaneously. The width of each band represents the number of job postings flowing from an industry through a job title into a required skill. This makes it easy to see where demand is concentrated across the entire hiring pipeline at once.

Key Insights

  • Data Analysts is the dominant role across all industries, accounting for the majority of flow through the diagram.
  • Power BI is the single most demanded skill across all industries and titles.
  • Computer Programming and Management Consulting drive the highest volume of postings overall.
  • Life Sciences appears as a skill in Computer Programming firms, suggesting biotech/pharma adjacent data roles are growing in that sector.

Implications for Job Seekers

Focus on Data Analyst roles as the primary entry point. These represent the largest share of available positions across every major industry.

Job Title Demand by Industry - Heatmap

Why This Chart?

A heatmap is ideal for showing a two-dimensional comparison. Here we cross job titles against industries, with color intensity representing the number of postings. Darker cells immediately draw attention to where demand is highest.

Key Insights

  • Data Analysts in Computer Programming is the single hottest combination with 531 postings. By far the most in-demand role-industry pairing.
  • Data Analysts dominate across every industry, confirming they are the most sought-after data role regardless of sector.
  • Data Modelers show surprisingly strong demand in Computer Programming (122) and Computer Systems Design (61), suggesting these industries need professionals who can architect data infrastructure.
  • Public Accountants shows near-zero demand for most titles, confirming it is a niche sector for data roles.
  • Management Consulting shows relatively balanced demand across titles compared to other industries, suggesting consultancies hire a broader range of data professionals.

Implications for Job Seekers

If you are targeting a Data Analyst role, Computer Programming and Management Consulting firms offer the most opportunities. If you specialize in Data Modeling, Computer Programming is your primary target market.

Text Mining & Classification

The text mining of the job descriptions in the dataset was used to explore the difference between ML/DS-intensive roles and traditional BI roles. Supervised classification models then set out to predict whether a BI role is ML/DS-intensive using structured features. A logistic regression model and random forest model were compared using confusion matrices. Salary band and degree requirements were determined to be signifiers of true data-science roles.

KMeans Clustering Analysis

The unsupervised learning method was generated to identify hidden patterns amongst the dataset. The clusters produced by the model could be compared to NAICS industries as a way to identify possible classifications.
